# Phase 40.04: Improvement Decision Closure and Feedback Loop Handoff

## Purpose
Define the improvement decision closure and feedback loop handoff process so that evaluated recommendations conclude with explicit decisions, downstream ownership, and traceable feedback into future operational learning and roadmap governance cycles.

## Scope
This phase documents:
- closure expectations for evaluated recommendations
- handoff requirements for approved follow-up actions
- disposition requirements for deferred or rejected recommendations
- feedback loop expectations into future learning cycles
- ownership and tracking requirements after governance decisions
- auditability requirements for decision closure and feedback continuity

This phase does not implement delivery tracking tools, execution boards, or organizational performance reporting.

## Objectives
1. Ensure recommendation decisions conclude with explicit and recorded dispositions.
2. Require ownership handoff for approved actions and tracked follow-up obligations.
3. Preserve rationale for deferred, rejected, or monitoring-only outcomes.
4. Feed governance decisions back into the continuous improvement system for learning continuity.
5. Maintain auditable closure records linking recommendations to downstream outcomes.

## Closure Triggers
Decision closure should occur when a recommendation has been:
- approved for roadmap or implementation planning
- approved for operational or policy update
- deferred pending future conditions
- rejected as unsupported, out of scope, or not beneficial
- retained for monitoring without immediate action
- escalated to a higher authority for final disposition

## Required Closure Record
Each closed recommendation record should include:
- recommendation identifier
- final decision date
- decision outcome
- decision rationale
- approving or deciding authority
- receiving owner, if applicable
- required follow-up actions
- review date or re-entry trigger, if deferred or monitoring-only
- references to linked roadmap, policy, or operational artifacts

## Handoff Requirements
Where action is approved, the handoff should define:
- accountable owner
- delivery or follow-up expectation
- required constraints or guardrails
- dependency considerations
- expected validation or success criteria
- escalation path if follow-up stalls or conditions change

## Deferred and Rejected Outcomes
If a recommendation is deferred or rejected, the closure process should record:
- the reason for non-approval
- whether additional evidence could change the outcome
- whether the recommendation should be revisited later
- whether related signals should continue to be monitored
- who authorized the disposition

## Feedback Loop Expectations
Governance decision outcomes should feed back into the operational learning system so that it remains clear:
- which recommendations produced concrete changes
- which recommendations stalled or were declined
- what evidence proved sufficient or insufficient
- what recurring themes indicate future architecture, policy, or process improvements
- when prior decisions should be re-opened due to new evidence

## Continuity Controls
The feedback loop should minimize loss of context across cycles by preserving:
- linkage to the original learning input
- linkage to evaluation and prioritization records
- linkage to downstream actions or monitoring obligations
- linkage to later reviews, reopenings, or superseding decisions

## Auditability Requirements
The process must preserve:
- what final decision was made
- why that decision was made
- who made or approved it
- where ownership transferred
- what future obligations remained
- how the decision re-entered or informed later operational learning

## Acceptance Criteria
- Closure requirements for recommendation decisions are documented.
- Handoff requirements for approved actions are explicit.
- Deferred, rejected, and monitoring-only outcomes are governed.
- Feedback loop continuity into future learning cycles is defined.
- Auditability requirements for closure and handoff are clear.
