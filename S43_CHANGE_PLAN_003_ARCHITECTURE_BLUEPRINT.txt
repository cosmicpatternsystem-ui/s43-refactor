1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This document defines the official architecture blueprint for S43_CHANGE_PLAN_003.

Its purpose is to establish the structural direction of CP-003 before any code mutation begins.

CP-003 is not a feature patch.

CP-003 is an architecture scaffold whose role is to prepare S43 for:

- modular safety
- controlled autonomy
- AI supervision
- audit-grade decision tracing
- portfolio intelligence
- hidden-signal analysis
- future multi-exchange expansion
- future commercial packaging

------------------------------------------------------------
2. CP-003 STRATEGIC ROLE
------------------------------------------------------------

CP-003 must create structure, not uncontrolled behavior.

CP-003 exists to introduce architectural boundaries and future-ready modules while preserving the locked verified baseline.

CP-003 must not:

- activate live trading
- grant exchange write access
- authorize wallet withdrawals
- allow AI to execute trades directly
- replace the canonical baseline
- create a fourth codebase
- perform a full rewrite

CP-003 must:

- define module boundaries
- establish contracts and interfaces
- isolate safety laws
- isolate autonomy controls
- isolate AI supervision pathways
- isolate execution approval rules
- make future extension safer

------------------------------------------------------------
3. ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

S43 must evolve through layered modularization.

The architecture direction is:

Input -> Analysis -> Intelligence -> Safety -> Approval -> Execution Boundary -> Receipt -> Audit

This means S43 should move toward a system where no single module has uncontrolled authority.

Every meaningful action must pass through structure.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. MODULE RESPONSIBILITIES
------------------------------------------------------------

SafetyLawEngine:
Owns hard safety laws that cannot be bypassed by AI, autonomy mode, or strategy logic.

AutonomyModeManager:
Controls whether the system is in observation mode, advisory mode, paper mode, restricted execution mode, or future approved live mode.

AISupervisorBridge:
Provides a supervised interface for AI analysis, explanation, suggestion, and classification without granting direct execution authority.

PortfolioBrain:
Represents portfolio-level awareness including allocation logic, exposure reasoning, concentration awareness, and capital preservation framing.

MarketRegimeEngine:
Classifies market states such as trend, chop, volatility expansion, compression, and abnormal conditions.

HiddenSignalEngine:
Searches for non-obvious market signals, liquidity anomalies, structural divergences, or asymmetric clues.

InvisibleRiskEngine:
Detects latent risk factors not obvious in naive signal logic, including correlation spikes, unstable conditions, timing fragility, and regime mismatch.

AsymmetricOpportunityScorer:
Scores opportunities by reward asymmetry, quality, conviction, and risk-adjusted attractiveness.

NoiseRejectionLayer:
Filters low-quality or noisy signals to reduce overreaction and false positives.

DiamondDecisionReceipt:
Creates explainable decision artifacts showing what the system saw, why it acted or refused, and which constraints applied.

AuditReceiptEngine:
Creates immutable-style operational records for verification, review, and governance evidence.

OwnerOverrideController:
Defines the owner-controlled authority boundary for future manual approvals or hard stops.

ExecutionApprovalPolicy:
Determines whether any candidate action is eligible to reach the execution boundary.

AdapterLayer:
Insulates the core architecture from external integrations such as exchanges, wallets, data sources, and AI providers.

ProductEditionManager:
Supports future differentiation between internal, private, premium, and commercial product editions.

CustomerProfileEngine:
Supports future rule variation by deployment profile, risk profile, or product edition without changing core safety laws.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. CONTROL BOUNDARIES
------------------------------------------------------------

S43 must obey strict architectural boundaries.

Safety Boundary:
No downstream component may override hard safety laws.

AI Boundary:
AI may analyze, classify, summarize, and explain.
AI may not directly place trades, move funds, or bypass approval rules.

Execution Boundary:
Execution must remain isolated behind explicit approval logic.
No analysis module may directly execute actions.

Owner Boundary:
Owner override must be explicit, logged, and policy-governed.

Integration Boundary:
Exchange APIs, wallet APIs, and external providers must remain behind adapters.

Audit Boundary:
Every material decision path should be receipt-capable.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. READ-ONLY VS MUTATING DOMAINS
------------------------------------------------------------

In the CP-003 architecture design, the preferred default is read-only behavior.

Read-only domains include:

- market observation
- signal analysis
- regime classification
- risk scoring
- portfolio reasoning
- AI explanation
- audit logging
- receipt generation
- simulation preparation

Mutating domains include, but are not limited to:

- order placement
- order cancellation
- leverage changes
- wallet transfers
- exchange write operations
- credential-sensitive state changes

During CP-003, mutating domains must remain disabled unless explicitly authorized in a future approved change-plan.
