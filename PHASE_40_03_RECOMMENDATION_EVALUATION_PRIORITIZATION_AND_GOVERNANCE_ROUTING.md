# Phase 40.03: Recommendation Evaluation, Prioritization, and Governance Routing

## Purpose
Define the recommendation evaluation, prioritization, and governance routing model so that validated operational learning inputs are assessed consistently, directed to the correct decision path, and prioritized according to impact, risk, urgency, and implementation scope.

## Scope
This phase documents:
- evaluation expectations for governance-ready recommendations
- prioritization dimensions and decision factors
- routing rules for different recommendation types
- handling of cross-domain or overlapping recommendations
- review outcome categories for governance consideration
- auditability requirements for evaluation and routing decisions

This phase does not implement portfolio tooling, execution planning systems, or automated prioritization engines.

## Objectives
1. Establish a consistent method for evaluating governance-ready recommendations.
2. Ensure recommendations are prioritized using explicit and reviewable decision factors.
3. Route recommendations to the correct governance authority or decision forum.
4. Reduce ambiguity when recommendations span roadmap, policy, operations, controls, or monitoring domains.
5. Preserve auditable records of how recommendations were evaluated, prioritized, and routed.

## Evaluation Inputs
Recommendation evaluation should consider:
- originating learning intake and supporting evidence
- validated problem statement
- affected capability, service, or control area
- user, operational, safety, or policy impact
- urgency and recurrence characteristics
- delivery complexity and dependency implications
- residual risk if no action is taken
- reversibility or mitigation alternatives

## Prioritization Dimensions
Recommendations should be prioritized using dimensions such as:
- impact severity
- likelihood or recurrence frequency
- user or operational exposure
- policy or safety sensitivity
- breadth of affected systems or workflows
- implementation effort or coordination cost
- time sensitivity
- risk reduction potential

## Governance Routing Categories
Recommendations may be routed to one or more paths such as:
- roadmap planning
- operational procedure update
- policy or control review
- reliability or resilience improvement
- observability enhancement
- dependency management review
- monitoring-only retention
- executive or exception escalation

## Routing Rules
The routing model should define:
- how primary ownership is assigned
- when multiple governance forums must coordinate
- when a recommendation should be split into separate actions
- when a recommendation should remain under observation instead of action
- when urgent handling overrides normal sequencing

## Review Outcome Categories
Evaluation should produce a recorded outcome such as:
- approved for planning
- approved for operational update
- approved for policy or control review
- deferred with rationale
- retained for monitoring
- rejected with rationale
- escalated for exception or executive review

## Cross-Domain Handling
Where recommendations span multiple domains, the process should document:
- primary accountable owner
- dependent reviewers or approvers
- sequencing constraints across domains
- conflict resolution expectations
- final authority for tie-breaking or escalation

## Prioritization Transparency
The process should make clear:
- why a recommendation received its priority level
- what assumptions influenced its ranking
- what tradeoffs were considered
- what changed if priority was later revised

## Auditability Requirements
The process must preserve:
- what recommendation was evaluated
- what evidence informed the evaluation
- how prioritization factors were applied
- where the recommendation was routed
- what outcome was recorded
- who made or approved the routing decision

## Acceptance Criteria
- Evaluation expectations for governance-ready recommendations are documented.
- Prioritization dimensions and routing categories are explicit.
- Cross-domain and urgent-handling rules are defined.
- Review outcome categories are documented.
- Auditability requirements for evaluation, prioritization, and routing are clear.

## Exit Condition
This phase is complete when governance-ready recommendations can be consistently evaluated, prioritized, routed, and dispositioned through a documented and auditable decision model.
