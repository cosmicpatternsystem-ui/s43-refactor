# Phase 39.04: Stabilization Exit Criteria and Steady-State Handoff

## Purpose
Define the stabilization exit criteria and steady-state handoff contract so that post-release monitoring concludes only when release behavior is sufficiently understood, residual risks are explicitly accepted, and ownership transitions into normal operational governance are auditable and controlled.

## Scope
This phase documents:
- criteria for determining stabilization completion
- required evidence for recommending stabilization exit
- residual risk review expectations at handoff time
- ownership transition requirements into steady-state operations
- approval expectations for concluding stabilization
- auditability requirements for stabilization closure and handoff

This phase does not implement long-term operations tooling, service management workflows, or organizational staffing models.

## Objectives
1. Establish explicit criteria for ending the stabilization period.
2. Ensure stabilization exit decisions are based on evidence rather than elapsed time alone.
3. Require explicit review of residual risks, open issues, and accepted constraints.
4. Formalize transition of responsibility from stabilization oversight to steady-state ownership.
5. Preserve auditable records of why stabilization was closed and how handoff occurred.

## Exit Criteria Categories
Stabilization exit evaluation should consider:
- sustained service reliability within accepted bounds
- absence of unresolved critical or recovery-grade instability
- acceptable behavior of policy and safety enforcement signals
- acceptable trend direction for anomalies, regressions, and user-impact indicators
- sufficient monitoring confidence and observability integrity
- completion or disposition of required corrective actions
- explicit review of remaining constraints, exceptions, and residual risks

## Evidence Requirements
A stabilization exit recommendation should include evidence such as:
- summary of key stabilization-period signals
- confirmation of threshold and alert outcomes
- disposition of material incidents, anomalies, or escalations
- status of corrective and preventive actions
- residual risk summary
- statement of monitoring readiness for steady-state ownership
- references to supporting reports, dashboards, or review records

## Residual Risk Review
Before stabilization exit is approved, remaining risks should be reviewed for:
- user impact potential
- safety or policy sensitivity
- dependency fragility
- operational workarounds still in effect
- unresolved observability limitations
- likelihood of recurrence under expected operating conditions

Residual risks must be explicitly accepted, assigned, or escalated.

## Steady-State Handoff Requirements
The handoff into steady-state operations should define:
- receiving owner or operational authority
- active constraints that remain in place
- open follow-up items and deadlines
- monitoring obligations that continue after stabilization
- known exceptions and approved risk acceptances
- escalation path if deferred issues worsen after handoff

## Approval Expectations
Stabilization closure should require explicit approval from authorized reviewers who can confirm:
- exit criteria were evaluated
- evidence is sufficient
- unresolved issues are either closed, accepted, or tracked
- steady-state ownership is prepared to assume responsibility

## Closure Record
The stabilization closure record should capture:
- stabilization period identifier
- exit recommendation date
- summary decision
- approved exit criteria assessment
- residual risk disposition
- receiving owner
- approver identities
- follow-up obligations after handoff

## Auditability Requirements
The process must preserve:
- what evidence supported stabilization exit
- what residual risks remained
- who accepted or approved those risks
- when ownership transferred
- what obligations remained open after handoff
- whether post-handoff issues triggered re-evaluation

## Acceptance Criteria
- Stabilization exit criteria categories are documented.
- Evidence requirements for exit recommendation are explicit.
- Residual risk review expectations are defined.
- Steady-state handoff and approval requirements are documented.
- Auditability requirements for stabilization closure are clear.

## Exit Condition
This phase is complete when stabilization can be closed only through a documented, evidence-based, and auditable decision that transfers responsibility into steady-state operations with explicit residual risk handling.
